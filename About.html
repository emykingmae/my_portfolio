A little about me

view resume

I’m Justin, a Product Designer currently based in Brooklyn.


Drawing on a background in project management, I’m a designer who understands how to identify the problem at hand, communicate in a way the entire team can align around, then build a user experience that not only solves user problems, but delights.

Here are some of my core design beliefs:

I believe there is significant business value in implementing design systems across organizations, and that everyone has the ability to (and should) wear the designer hat sometimes.

I believe in collaboration, and in the power of a great team that listens to each other.

I believe in talking to our users (a lot).

I believe the best design solutions are built on the foundations of qualitative user research, project constraints, and lots of communication with and support from colleagues.

I believe in an iterative approach.

I believe technology can make our lives easier and more enjoyable, and I’m interested in how we do that in a way that’s inclusive and serves everyone.

Before Design

Life is never a straight line. This is why we should never be afraid to accept new dreams and goals for ourselves.





My creativity and desire to solve problems for people have always been a unique piece of who I am. I’m at my best when I’m in a state of creating and when I’m finding solutions to difficult problems.

At Penguin Random House I first grew as a creative problem solver and as a team leader. I experienced the joy in bringing a project from an idea to a final product and seeing the world interact with it. As a project manager I learned how to navigate many personalities, approach their problems with empathy, and then work with them to find answers. But when I looked around at the projects I was involved in I began asking, “What piece of this equation most interests me?” It was the designers whose work I most wanted to jump into.

I quickly discovered the world of design was so vast, filled with so many kinds of problem solvers and creative people doing remarkable work across industries. I wanted in.

This is when I started developing my passion for the discipline of UX Design.

I enrolled in Bloc/Thinkful’s UX Design and Frontend immersive bootcamp and it was there that I found the structure I was missing to level up my design and computer science education. I evolved as a professional and captured a new dream of helping to build the user experiences of the future.

I believe that no matter what we do with our days, staying curious is the key to unlocking so much of our own potential and doing great work. I hope you and I get the chance to work together. Everyone has their own story to tell and I’m interested in learning about yours and what your ideas are for building great tech.



Thanks for reading!
